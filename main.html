Audrey Hepburn

Born:1929- Iconic actress &humanitarian

Quick Summary

Audrey Kathleen Hepburn (née Ruston; 4 May 1929 – 20 January 1993) was a British[a] actress. Recognised as a film and fashion icon, she was ranked by the American Film Institute as the third-greatest female screen legend from the Classical Hollywood cinema, inducted into the International Best Dressed Hall of Fame List, and is one of a few entertainers who have won competitive Academy, Emmy, Grammy and Tony Awards.

"Noting is impossibolle,the word itself says 'I'm possible"

Did you know?

Audrey Kathleen Hepburn studied ballet at the Arnhem Conservatory during the war. By 1944, Hepburn was performing ballet to raise money to support the resistance. She studied with Sonia Gaskell in Amsterdam from 1945 to 1948 and then with Marie Rambert in London.

Denzel Washington

Born on December 28, 1954

Quick Summary

Denzel Washington is an acclaimed American actor, director, and producer, widely regarded as one of the greatest actors of the 21st century. He is known for his powerful, dramatic roles and has received numerous accolades, including two Academy Awards and a Tony Award.

"Do what you have to do, to do what you want to do"

Did you know?

Academy Awards: Washington has won two Oscars: Best Supporting Actor for his role as a defiant ex-slave soldier in the Civil War film Glory (1989). Best Actor for his portrayal of a corrupt and charismatic police detective in the crime thriller Training Day (2001), becoming the second African-American actor to win this award.

Meryl Streep

Born on June 22, 1949

Quick Summary

Meryl Streep is widely considered one of the greatest and most versatile actresses in cinematic history, celebrated for her mastery of accents and transformative performances.

"The great gift of human beings is that we have the power of empathy."

Did you know?

Record Nominations: She holds the record for the most Academy Award nominations of any actor, male or female, with 21 total nominations. Major Awards: Streep has won three Academy Awards: Best Supporting Actress for Kramer vs. Kramer (1979) and Best Actress for Sophie's Choice (1982) and The Iron Lady (2011).
